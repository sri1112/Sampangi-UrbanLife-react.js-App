    Here are some simple and clear answers you can use in an interview, especially when discussing your day-to-day activities as a Java developer:

    Example Answers

    1. Checking Emails: "In the morning, the first thing I do is check my emails. This helps me know what tasks I need to focus on and if there are any updates from my team or clients."
    2. Reviewing the Storyboard: "After checking emails, I look at the storyboard. It shows the current status of my work tasks and any new assignments. This way, I can plan my day effectively."
    3. Daily Standup Meetings: "I attend a daily standup meeting with my team. In this meeting, we share what we did yesterday, what we will do today, and if we have any problems. It helps us stay connected and on track."
    4. Coding and Development: "Most of my day is spent writing code. I'm typically working on specific tasks assigned to me, such as creating new features or fixing bugs. I also write tests to make sure everything works correctly."
    5. Helping Others: "If any junior developers have questions, I help them with their problems. I enjoy sharing what I know and helping others learn."
    6. Filling Timesheets: "At the end of the day, I fill out my timesheet. This helps track my work hours and keeps the project organized."
    7. Final Email Check: "Before I finish my work and log off, I check my emails one last time. This ensures I dont miss any important messages before the day ends."

    Tips for Interview

    • Speak clearly and confidently.
    • Be honest about your experiences.
    • Use examples from your past work to support your answers.
    • Keep your answers brief and focused.
